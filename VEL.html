Virtual Embodiement Lab, Cornell University

Professor Andrea Stevenson Won

The Virtual Embodiement Lab at Cornell University conducts 4-6 diverse research projects a semester. The research projects range from communication experiments for better understanding how people communicate in a virtual space, to projects performing medical research on the effects of perception in a virtual space on someone's physical pain. The VEL is an exciting research lab in which I am a world builder, avatar coder, and research assistant

  • Distance Team, Spring 2017

    The Distance study was a study that evolved from Professor Won's Distance study in the Communication department at Stanford University. This study researched how participants would estimate their physical distance from confederates while speaking over text message. The study found that when confederates were texting in a friendly way (responding quickly, asking questions, using emojis, etc) the participants would estimate that person's physical distance from them (in miles) to be significantly closer than when confederates texted in a cold way (short, flat, slow).

    This initial study was then moved and built in the virtual space in Cornell's lab. My role as a world builder required me to build models in Autodesk 3ds Max and import them into the VR Platform HiFidelity. I also worked in human experiments as a researcher and a confederate, and helped write the script and perform data analysis on the results.

  • Physics Team, Fall 2017

    The Physics study is a research study focused on how we can educate grade school students about the laws of physics in virtual reality. My role as world builder and avatar interaction coder support the team by creating virtual environments in which the team can perform experiments. We use Unity for modeling and program in C#. This project is in its infancy.
