Mambo! I'm Annie, a recent Cornell graduate with a B.A. in Information Science.

I believe that businesses have the power to shape the future through designing technology for social impact. My favorite quote is: "The best way to predict the future is to design it." I am a web designer who's passionate about making the world more accessible to all through intelligent, inspired solutions that understand and empathize with their user.

This site is a place for me to share my projects and to serve as a creative development outlet. This site is owned by myself, with the use of our friend Bootstrap :-)

Design

One of my major concentrations is in User Experience. I have been trained in and have experience with Human-Computer Interaction and UX Design and research. I am minoring in Game Design and was a designer for the award winning slide-until-blocked puzzle game, Operation Bitwise at the Game Design Initiative at Cornell's showcase in May 2017. I also am a research assitant in the Virtual Embodiement Lab at Cornell University where I design models in Unity for human-subject experiments in VR using the Oculus Rift and the HTC Vive. This semester I am on the Lunar Phases team, a physics education research project funded by Oculus Rift.

Develop

My other concentration is in Interactive Technologies. I have experience with Object Oriented Programming & Data Structures as well as Web Programming including the development of this site. I am proficient in the programming languages: Java, Python, MatLab, Arduino, C#, HTML, CSS, PHP, MySQL, JavaScript, jQuery, and have used Bootstrap and other development frameworks. I have experience with Linux/UNIX operating systems and bash scripting. I am currently in a Rapid Prototyping & Physical Computing course in which my team is designing and building a theremin contolled light display.

Dialogue

I also have studied Swahili for three years at Cornell, and have a large interest in the study of the many forms of discrimination and oppression in the United States. I am a registered Citizen Potawatomi Nation tribal member, and my family and I value and study our traditions. I am currently participating in the Intergroup Dialogue Project and consider myself an ally to oppressed communities. I am passionate about educating myself about the lives of others in order to become a better contributor to the world and to the people around me. I believe we have strength in our differences.
