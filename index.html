My UX Journey



Arcane Tectonics

Arcane Tectonics is a match-three mobile game where the grid of tiles being moved around are pieces of the ground being moved by Mika’s magical powers. Enemies and other game pieces stand on the ground, so they are moved around as the ground tiles move. To clear each level, the player must use Mika’s powers to slide tiles around and create matches to destroy all of the enemies on the board.

Tasti App

For the 2017 CHI Student Design Competition's theme of Leveling the Playing Field: designing technology for populations that are usually left out of HCI, my team and I as part of Cornell University's INFO 3450: Human Computer Interaction Design class, explored the problem of food insecurity for students with dietary restrictions.

Sweets By Sarah K

Sweets By Sarah K is a small cookie business based in Spokane, WA. In the fall of 2017 Sarah K asked me to make her website and create content for it. We have a wonderful relationship and I sure love her cookies!

Operation Bitwise

Operation Bitwise is a slide-until-blocked strategic puzzle game. Players control the two viruses, Vi and Russ, who must cooperatively interact with each other to 'hack' each level. Operation Bitwise won 'Best Gameplay' at the 2017 GDIAC Showcase and was presented atBits On Our Minds, the premier annual showcase for Cornell student projects in cutting-edge digital technology.

Girl4Girl

Girl4Girl is founded on three pillars: tutoring, mentorship and community. It is an online startup aimed at instantly connecting females in STEM to their female peers for tutoring, questions, and advice. We aim to help women in STEM develop confidence through each other to maintain the growth of female representation in science and math and fight the male established stigma about females in STEM.

Website Redesign

During my internship at Caxy I contributed to two website redesign projects by participating in daily meetings with clients, conducting and analyzing user and stakeholder interviews. I analyzed user data to assess pain points and create heuristic evaluations to make informed adjustments to information architecture and UI design.

Charlotte the Spiderbot

Charlotte is a robot that's controled by two sonar sensors. One sensor controls forward and backward movement, while the other controls left and right movement. The left and right sensors also control "legs" that hold markers, diagonal legs lift and release when either the left and right sensor are triggered.
